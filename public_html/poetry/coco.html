Coco

October 07, 2014

Color Poem for Literary Genres

As fairytales ended,
That’s how I found you.
But more honestly said,
You found me.

Liquid chestnut ran from your eye
As you leapt at me
Joyously heading me home.

Chocolate became your
Poison and your perseverance,
Your skill, name, claim to fame.

From humble cardboard beginnings,
You came.
That tan flag,
The helicopter propeller you flew
Guided us, your charges,
Home.

Sturdy, unstreamlined, enduring, and kind,
Miles of shell laden sand removed,
Gigantic hairy beasts kept at bay,
Children caught, tears turned to warmth,
Soil strewn joyously to the sky.

There, dependable you were.
The brown and black princess,
Queen of the Nile.

Leave blue to the sky,
It envies your richness.
